24 Hour Access

Train at 5am. Train at midnight. Your schedule, your gym. Available as an add-on to any Standard membership.

The Pitch

Life in Marin doesn't run on a class schedule. Early mornings, late nights, those weird middle-of-the-day windows when you suddenly have 45 minutes free - 24 hour access means you never miss a session.

This is for the members who want to put in extra work. The ones who come to class and then want to spend another 20 minutes on their snatch. The ones who travel for work and need to make up a WOD on a Sunday morning before anyone else is awake.

The Process

HOW IT WORKS

Getting 24 hour access is straightforward. There are two things you need to do - neither takes long.

01
📋
Step 01
Sign the After-Hours Agreement
A quick liability waiver acknowledging the solo-use rules and your responsibility while training unsupervised. It takes about 2 minutes to complete. Once signed, it stays on file - you only do this once.
02
🔑
Step 02
Get Your Key Fob
We program a key fob specifically to your membership. It grants you entry to the gym 24 hours a day, 7 days a week, 365 days a year. No app, no PIN - just tap and you're in. Pick it up at the front desk during any staffed hour.
The Rules

24 HOUR GUIDELINES

These rules exist to protect the equipment, the space, and your safety while no staff is present. They're not complex - just common sense for training solo in a shared space.

Rule 01
One Person at a Time
24 hour access is for solo training only. Do not bring guests, training partners, or anyone else into the gym during unstaffed hours. This is non-negotiable.
Rule 02
No Coaching or Instruction
You may not coach or instruct others during after-hours access. No "just showing a friend around." Coaching happens during class time with staff present.
Rule 03
No Dropping Weights
During after-hours training, no dropping barbells, dumbbells, or any equipment. This is a noise and safety requirement - neighbors and equipment both thank you.
Rule 04
Use Rubber Bumpers Only
Only rubber bumper plates may be used during unstaffed hours. Iron plates stay racked. If you need to go heavy, come to class where coaches can spot you.
Rule 05
Rack Your Weights
Leave the gym exactly as you found it. Everything back where it belongs - barbells, plates, kettlebells, boxes. The first staff member in shouldn't have to clean up your session.
Rule 06
No High-Risk Movements
Max effort lifts, box jumps to max height, and other high-risk movements are discouraged without supervision. Train smart - no PR attempts when you're solo.
Rule 07
Music at Reasonable Volume
You can play your own music, but keep it at a reasonable volume. The building has neighboring tenants and the community around us matters. Use headphones if you need it loud.
Rule 08
Secure the Door Behind You
When you enter and when you leave, verify the door is fully closed and locked. Do not hold the door for anyone - if they have access, they have a fob.
Rule 09
No Food or Drinks
Water is fine. Meals, protein shakes with strong smells, or anything that makes a mess stays outside. Keep the floor clean - it's a gym, not a kitchen.
Rule 10
Report Any Issues
Notice broken equipment, a light out, or anything that seems wrong? Text or email us immediately rather than waiting. Catching small problems early keeps the gym working for everyone.
Rule 11
Access Can Be Revoked
Violations of these guidelines result in immediate revocation of 24 hour access privileges. We keep this privilege available because members respect it. Help us keep it that way.
—
The Golden Rule
Train as if the morning coach is about to walk in. Because eventually they will - and they'll be able to tell whether you respected the space.
